The online place of popular music Exploring the impact of geography and social media on pop artists mainstream media attention

Artists have embraced social network sites such as MySpace, Facebook, and Twitter to connect directly to fans (Marwick & boyd, 2011). Part of the appeal of such sites is what Beer (2008) called the “perception of proximity”: fans feel more closely connected to artists since they can become “friends” and can receive messages that appear to (and sometimes actually do) come from the artists themselves.
